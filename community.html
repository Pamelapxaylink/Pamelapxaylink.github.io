I am grateful for the life I have and through faith I believe it is important to always help those in need. I did not have a lot growing up, so being able to volunteer my time at Ronald Mcdonald House taught me a lot. I was able to cook healthy meals for families who were going through hardships. Not only that, but during college I would devote my time to help oragnize and distribute food to families in need at the bulldog pantry. Also, I wanted experience at a hospital, so I volunteered at Clovis Community Hospital to see what it was like on a daily basis to care for ill patients. Life is too short to not be thankful. We as a society can come together to help imrpove the lives of others. With compassion and determination I will continue to give back to the central valley.
